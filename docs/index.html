Machine Learning for
Astroparticle Physics

A Crash-Course in Simulation-Based Inference

ICTP-SAIFR SAMMA 2026 PhD School — São Paulo, 2026 — Christoph Weniger (GRAPPA / University of Amsterdam)

  • Lecture 1a
    ML in Astroparticle Physics & Gravitational Waves
    A guided tour of where ML has actually landed in APP/GW research, organised around the scientific-method loop: theory, simulator, instrument, data, inference. Closes on the two ways to do Bayesian inference with a simulator — setting up Lec 1b.
    Overview DINGO IceCube LIGO Emulators SBI taxonomy
  • Lecture 1b
    Classic ABC — Three Demos on a Ball Throw
    A three-demo staircase: rejection ABC → add a summary statistic → KDE in (θ, x)-space. Live, slider-driven demos expose the two failures — summary identification and high-D density estimation — that motivate the rest of the school.
    ABC Rejection sampling Summary statistics KDE Curse of dimensionality
  • Lecture 2
    Building qφ(θ | x) — from χ² to Neural Densities
    One continuous story: parameterise p(θ | x) and make it more flexible as need demands. χ² as conditional Gaussian density estimation, overfitting and the basis-function problem, neural networks + SGD, heteroscedastic and flexible qφ(θ | x).
    χ² / MLE Basis functions Overfitting Neural networks SGD Gaussian-head NPE
    Hands-on session 1 notebook — PyTorch crash + Gaussian-head NPE Hands-on session 1 APP example — gravitational-wave chirp
  • Lecture 3
    Inference Heads — Flows, Flow Matching, Diffusion
    From qφ(θ | x) to a production-grade posterior. NPE, NRE, NLE and when each shines. Normalising flows in depth (change of variables, coupling layers, conditional flows), flow matching and diffusion as visual side-companions, and DINGO as the loud production example.
    NPE / NRE / NLE Normalising flows Coupling layers Flow matching Diffusion DINGO
  • Lecture 4
    Convolutional Networks & Learned Summary Statistics
    From raw observation x to a learned feature sψ(x) that feeds the inference head. Convolutions, channels, pooling, and architectures (LeNet, ResNet) as the building blocks of summary networks. The block that lets NPE swallow images, time series, and high-dimensional data without hand-crafted summaries.
    CNNs Channels & kernels Pooling ResNet Summary networks Embeddings
